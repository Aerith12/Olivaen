· My mother, Sfax, Tunisia

My grandfather never left Sfax. He was born near these trees, and he grew old beside them. Every morning, before the sun was fully up, he was already out there. Walking the rows, checking the soil, talking to them the way some people talk to God. He never used a single chemical. Not because he knew the science. Because he respected what was already there.

When he passed, he left my mother the land and nothing else. No instructions, no manual, no note. He didn't think he needed to. She had watched him her whole life. And so every October, she goes out the same way he did. Same hands, same earth.

I grew up watching her do that. What they built wasn't a business. It was a kind of faithfulness — to the land, to each other, to something they couldn't quite name. I am the one who finally named it. And I made a promise: when I put it in a bottle, it would be worthy of them. Every single drop.
